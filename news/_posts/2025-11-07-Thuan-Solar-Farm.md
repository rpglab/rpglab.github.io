* On November 7, 2025, eight of us visited a 300-MWp Solar Farm owned by TotalEnergies: <a href="/people/Xingpeng-Li/" class="" target="_blank">Xingpeng Li</a>, <a href="/people/Jesus-SilvaRodriguez/" class="" target="_blank">Jesus Silva-Rodriguez</a>, <a href="/people/Rida-Fatima/" class="" target="_blank">Rida Fatima</a>, <a href="/people/Ann-Mary-Toms/" class="" target="_blank">Ann Mary Toms</a>, <a href="/people/Hassan-Zahid-Butt/" class="" target="_blank">Hassan-Zahid-Butt</a>, <a href="/people/Fan-Jiang/" class="" target="_blank">Fan Jiang</a>, <a href="/people/Linhan-Fang/" class="" target="_blank">Linhan Fang</a>, and <a href="/people/Haoxiang-Wan/" class="" target="_blank">Haoxiang Wan</a>. 

* Many thanks to our tour host <a href="/people/Thuan-Pham/" class="" target="_blank">Dr. Thuan Pham</a> - a former PhD student from our group.

* It was a great experience to visit a large-scale solar farm in the field, especially for us who often simulate and model solar power in our research.

* We had a close look at the solar panels, single-axis trackers, power cables, disconnectors, inverters, transformers, and the substation (circuit breakers, switches, capacitor banks, transformers, relays, SCADA software system, onsite backup batteries).

* This solar farm has a dedicated 345 kV transmission line that connects it to the ERCOT 345 kV transmission network.

* After the tour, we had a group lunch at Puerto Vallarta - a nice Texan-Mexican food resturant.
